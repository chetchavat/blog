Gerland's Grand Market (RIP) Deli Worker

Sugar Land, Texas Summer 2007

This job paid like six bucks an hour, and was pretty slow most of the time. Unfortunately the shifts were six hours long, and I always worked the closing shift. Also, I had to wear a white dress shirt and black slacks all the time, which was weird. Currently, as a 30 year old, I have never worn a white dress shirt with black slacks to work since this job.

I cut deli meats and cheese using a deli slicer. I never hurt myself, and I never really understood the different measurements people wanted. My cold cuts were either real thin, regularish size, or hella thick. If you said something like 0 or 3 or whatever, I would nod and cut off a guess.

"How does this look?"

Most of the time, it was fine.

A real highlight of this job was coming in and starting the dinner batch of rotisserie chickens. I would go to the butcher section, grab a box of whole fryers from the freezer and take them back to the deli. Then I would rip out the fat from the inside of the chicken and throw it away.
What followed is what could be called "liberal seasoning". Then I would skewer the chickens on the rotisserie rack and put them in the rotating oven. I enjoyed that I had to do this every time I came into work, it was a nice routine to start the shift, gutting these chickens and covering them in lemon pepper.

The Deli was staffed by older minorities, and two younger minorities (of which I was one). A lot of the time, I worked with an older Hispanic lady who thought my name was Isaac the entire time I was there. The other people working at the store were younger, it's just the Deli that was filled with older folks.
